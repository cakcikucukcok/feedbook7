The basis for most maps is imagery. Imagery and GIS: Best Practices for Extracting Information from Imagery shows how imagery can be integrated successfully into maps and GIS projects. Readers will learn how GIS can be used to derive value from imagery through enhanced visualizations and extraction and analysis of information. Plus readers will learn how to efficiently manage and serve imagery datasets. The authors share practical considerations and lessons learned from real world applications. With more than 200 full-color illustrations, this reference guide helps readers use image datasets that best satisfy their requirements to get the job done.
