Meno - Plato | Meno (/ ˈ m iː n oʊ /; Greek: Μένων, Menōn) is a Socratic dialogue scripted by Plato. It appears to attempt to determine the definition of virtue , or arete , meaning virtue in general, rather than particular virtues, such as justice or temperance.

The Internet Classics Archive | Meno by Plato | Plato: Meno. Plato’s Meno introduces aspects of Socratic ethics and Platonic epistemology in a fictional dialogue that is set among important political events and cultural concerns in the last years of Socrates’ life. It begins as an abrupt, prepackaged debater’s challenge from Meno about whether virtue can be taught, and quickly becomes an open and inconclusive search for the essence of this elusive “virtue,” or human goodness in general.
