Midnight Transit is the second book of poems in The Golden Passage, a trilogy of by internationally known poet Charlotte Brady. Throughout the series, Brady boldly faces existential dilemmas that lie within each of us, and releases profound responses that sweep across our minds and make us see ourselves and the world around us more clearly. Her Socratic method of asking pointed questions combined with her lyrical use of language create a safe harbor for traveling beyond the boundaries of our minds and increasing our understanding of the human condition. Brady confronts topics such as intolerance, fear, and suffering with a steady gaze and an open heart. She discusses nature, love, and happiness with fresh understanding and memorable wisdom. In Midnight Transit, Brady focuses on the self in transition.
